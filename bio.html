About Amiyah Black

Amiyah Black comes from the Bronx, NY. She began her training at City Stage Dance Academy where she studied art forms like ballet, hip hop, tap, jazz, contemporary, salsa, and acro. She previously trained at The Ailey School's Junior Division, Level 7, and her training currently resides at LaGuardia High School of the Arts and MOVE|NYC|. During Amiyah's summers she spent her time at the Juilliard Summer Intensive(2024, 2025), under Alicia Graf Mack and Melissa Toogood.


Amiyah has performed in the Thanksgiving Day Parade in November 2022, 2024, and 2025. She was able to work with artists such as Taylor Momsen, Bishop Briggs in Netflix's “Wednesday” float, & Jordan Sparks in Toys R Us float. Amiyah made her New York City Center debut performing in Dance Against Cancer in 2024, choreography by Earl Mosley. She has also performed with Josh Groben at the Find Your Light Gala.


In 2025, Amiyah joined the LaGuradia Dance Ensemble at LaGuardia High School of the Arts. During her time in the Ensemble, she was recieved the opportunity to perform at Teens Take the Met and The Shed. She also performed in The FAME Documentary recreating the Taxi Scene.


Lastly, in 2023, 2024, and 2025, Amiyah performed in the MOVE|NYC| Young Professionals Ensemble Concert. Her time at MOVE provided her with the opportunity to work with Roderick George, Dual Rivet, Jamal Callendar, Shonica Gooden, Kamile Upshaw, and Kyrstal Mackie. She recieved the privillge to peform repertory by Marco Goecke and the Bill T. Jones Dance Company.
